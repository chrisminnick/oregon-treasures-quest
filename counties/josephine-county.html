The Oregon Caves

Call it Oregon's very own Cave of Wonders! Buried within the Siskiyou Mountains in Josephine County you'll find The Oregon Caves National Monument and Preserve‚Äîa labyrinth of bends, twists, and treasures that will truly allow you to explore a mountain from the inside and out. A hunter named Elijah Davidson came across the cave in 1874 while chasing after his dog who went in after a bear, and the cave went on to become the state's first national monument in 1909. Visit the "Marble Halls of Oregon" for yourself to see the colorful rocks of the cave that appear painted from the eons of acidic water that seeped into them. Tip: if you are super adventurous, check out the "Off Trail Caving Tour" opportunity and be sure to schedule ahead to secure your tour slot.
